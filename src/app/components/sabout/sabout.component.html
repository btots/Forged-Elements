Hi, I'm Ben Tosto

My "shop" in Australia.

I’ve always loved making things. As a kid, I played a lot with clay and Lego. When I was old enough to manage tools, I took an interest in woodwork, and spent a summer in my early 20’s working with my uncle in Virginia as a carpenter. I really enjoy woodwork, but when I had a chance to try metalwork, I fell head over heels for it. I first became enamored with metalwork at Principia College while doing machinist work for the solar car team. But it wasn’t until 2013 while living in Australia that I got to explore ironwork on my own terms.

A friend had given me a book that catalogued all sorts of third-world industrial practices in use during the 70’s, and several chapters of it were dedicated to different forms of metalwork. I was blown away at the ingenuity and simplicity of the techniques I learned there, and I decided to give it a go myself!

A few trips to the local junk yard later, I had my very own brake drum forge, railroad track anvil, tarpaulin bellows, and charcoal retort set up. Though I spent exponentially more time building this setup than I spent forging with it, the experience taught me a lot of valuable lessons. In any case, I was hooked.

The power hammers at Orion Forge in Bend, OR.

The following year, my wife and I moved to Bend, OR, and serendipitously I found myself in a small community with a high density of world-class blacksmiths. I immediately joined the Central Oregon Metal Arts Guild, and began attending Northwest Blacksmithing Association classes and conferences. Through these groups, I came to be acquainted with some of the most talented ironworkers in the country (and a few from Hungary and Italy!). After half a year of struggling on my own in an unused stable outside of town, I decided it was time to get serious about my craft.

From 2015 to 2017, I worked for Orion Forge and Dry Canyon Forge in Bend under the masterful guidance and direction of Hunter Dahlberg, Kellen Bateham, and Joe Elliott. The experience I gained working them was invaluable, and propelled my personal work into professional success.

Trying to blacksmith in my garage back in Oregon.

In April, 2018 my wife and I, looking forward to having kids, moved again to be closer to family - this time to Northern Michigan in a beautiful home on 17 acres adjoining Sleeping Bear National Park. From here the forgelight glows once more.

My passion is in custom furniture - the joining of wood and metal is a uniquely raw combination of textures, shapes, and colors that I never get tired of seeing juxtaposed - but I’ll always have a soft spot in my heart for the forged hand tools I learned my trade making.
